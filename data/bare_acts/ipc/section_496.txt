A person with fraudulent intention going through the ceremony of being married knowing that he is not thereby lawfully married

Description of IPC Section 496
According to section 496 of Indian penal code, Whoever, dishonestly or with a fraudulent intention, goes through the ceremony of being married, knowing that he is not thereby lawfully married, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.


IPC 496 in Simple Words
If someone dishonestly or fraudulently undergoes a marriage ceremony, knowing they are not lawfully married, they can be punished with imprisonment for up to seven years and may also be fined.

Punishment: 7 Years + Fine
